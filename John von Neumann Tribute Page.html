John von Neumann

The man who revolutionized many fields.

Portrait of John von Neumann.

Here's a timeline of John von Neumann's life:

  • 1903 - Born in Budapest, Hungary.
  • 1919 - Fled with his family from the communist regime in Hungary.
  • 1923 - Published the paper "The Introduction of Transfinite Ordinals", which expanded on the idea of ordinal numbers.
  • 1926 - Did postdoctoral work under David Hillbert, a german mathmaticien, for a year.
  • 1925 - Received his degree in chemical engineering from the Swiss Federal Institute.
  • 1926 - Received his doctorate in mathematics from the Swiss Federal Institute.
  • 1927 - Started work as a private lecturer in the University of Berlin.
  • 1929 - Transfered to the University of Hamberg as a private lecturer.
  • 1932 - Published the book "The Mathematical Foundations of Quantum Mechanics" which pushed foward on the ideas of quantum states.
  • 1932 - Published a proof on the quasi-ergodic hypothesis.
  • 1933 - Married Mariette Koevesi and eventually had a child together.
  • 1937 - Divorced with his wife. Their seperation was amicable.
  • 1938 - Married Klara Dan, a childhood sweetheart. This marriage lasted the rest of Neumann's life.
  • 1943 - Began to work on the Manhattan Project, US's first atomic bomb.
  • 1944 - Published the book "Theory of Games and Economic Behavior", a book he collaborated on with Oskar Morgenstern.
  • 1944 - Started spending a good portion of his time as a consultant for the US goverment and it's industry.
  • 1944 - Participated in the construction of the ENIAC.
  • 1954 - Became a member of the Atomic Energy Commission and worked on the policy of nuclear deterrence.
  • 1955 - Diagnosed with bone cancer.
  • 1956 - Received the Enrico Fermi award.
  • 1957 - Died at the age of 53, in Washington, United States.
